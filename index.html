Fatality in States

The fatality rates in the states in Australia does not match the counts of deaths within them. Eventhough in Tasmania and Northern Territory, the fatality counts are among the lowest within the different states, they are among the highest for fatality rates. This is due to the low population of people in the state and maybe evident to the difficult terrain and roads within those states. 

The fatality rates for Western Australia, South Australia and Queensland are similar and close to the national average of 0.3875 deaths per 10,000 people. With these states  making up majority of the population within Australia, they represent of the general safety of roads within Australia. Therefore, tourist and drivers in Australia should drive with extra precaution within Tasmania and Northern Territory  as the "chance" of vehicular related deaths.

Fatalities across Time

Fatality rates have been dropping from 1989 to 2020. This shows that the general safety and quality of roads within Australia have improved since 1989. All of the states other than Northern Territory fall below 2 fatalities per 10,000 people. This shows that in Northern Territory the roads are generally more unsafe compared to the rest.

Who make up the fatalities?

From 1989 to 2020, the younger age group (17-25) make up majority of the deaths at the start and the age group (40-64) are ranked the highest. Men make up majority of the death counts each year as well.

For each state, the Driver makes up majority of the deaths throughout the years with the passenger ranking second.
